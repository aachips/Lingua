About Kitchen LinguaDownload PDF - Creative Brief

Kitchen Lingua strives to be an online learning hub for the Ladino language.Ladino, or Judeo-Spanish, is one of the many endangered languages that may have been brought back from extinction. This is an experiment to make a blog and graphic design business from a revival of an endangered language.

Ladino is a Jewish language that originated with the Sephardim, who were exiled from Spain in 1492. It is based on Old Spanish, with influences from Hebrew and Turkish. Ladino was typically not spoken in public, but in the home, particularly in the kitchen.

When a language dies, we lose a library of Science, Art, ways of living in the world, and generations of wisdom.

As a language of the kitchen, I believe the revival of Ladino can be a critical cultural element of Food Security in America, starting with education at the family and home level.

Objectives

  1. Bring Ladino revitalization to the United States
  2. Make learning Spanish fun and more meaningful
  3. Post helpful learning content for Judeo-Spanish
  4. Sell bookmarks, posters, and a rare custom coin
  5. Newsletter and vocabulary of the day on the Daily Palavra

Commitment


At Kitchen Lingua, we believe in lifelong learning. We also believe that learning a language can be a great way for generations in a family to bond. We believe that computer, web, and digital media technologies can be used to bring people together over common ideas, regardless of where they are in the world. Additionally, we believe that bilingual education is a great experience developmentally and for long-term life outcomes for both children and adults. Finally, we believe that learning a language can be a fun experience.

Brand

We create fun, interactive learning materials that are accessible to people of all ages and backgrounds. Our materials are available in English, Spanish, Hebrew, Turkish, and Ladino.

Our target audience is people who are either Jewish, have Jewish friends or family, have taken some Spanish courses in grade school, or want to learn alongside their kids who are taking those same courses. We believe that language learning can be fun and doesn't have to be painful.

We seek to collaborate with other Ladino content creators in other parts of the world, while mainly targeting an audience in the United States. This means using vibrant colors, approachable sans serif fonts, and symbols like the Hamsa Hand.

Logos - Meza d’Alegria - Tables of Joy

At the end of a large meal, a blessing in Ladino that is often said is Mezas di Alegria, or Tables of Joy. Something Kitchen Lingua aspires to do is make Judeo-Spanish a fun dinner table conversation topic. Who would you eat with at your Table of Joy? How often do you get to share a meal with loved ones?
